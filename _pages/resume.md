<p> build production AI systems and the technical narratives that help teams understand, trust, and adopt them.</p>
<p>My work sits at the intersection of AI engineering, applied architecture, technical strategy, and developer education. I've spent 20+ years building production software across AI/ML, speech and NLP, cloud-native platforms, Kubernetes, storage, virtualization, and backup/recovery. I'm focusing on shaping AI strategy, building proof-of-concepts for emerging products, and designing explainable AI systems with the governance needed for enterprise deployment.</p>
<p>I focus on Agentic AI, Retrieval-Augmented Generation, Graph RAG, Document RAG, multi-agent systems, AI memory, and the infrastructure patterns that make those systems reliable in the real world. That includes retrieval architectures that explain where answers came from, governance workflows that track data and model provenance, and systems that treat observability, lineage, and reproducibility as first-class requirements rather than decorative compliance confetti.</p>
<p>My background is both deeply technical and market-facing. I've built and trained models, designed AI architectures, contributed to open-source platforms, led engineering teams, launched SDKs, supported customer architecture reviews, created demos, built workshops, and partnered with product, sales, and marketing teams to turn complex technology into adoption. At Deepgram, I helped grow Python SDK usage from 10M to 118M calls and launched a Go SDK that generated 5M+ calls in its first month. At Symbl.ai, I founded the Go SDK and related integrations that helped support the company's first $60K edge-platform contract. Earlier, I managed a 20+ engineer team whose virtualization work helped grow a business unit from $880M to $2.18B.
I also teach what I learn. I have spoken at NVIDIA GTC, Devoxx, ODSC, DeveloperWeek, SCaLE, and WeAreDevelopers on explainable AI, adaptive RAG, agentic systems, model quantization, multimodal ML, voice cloning, and small language model training.</p>
<p>What motivates me is building AI that can survive contact with production: systems that are observable, measurable, explainable, and aligned with real customer needs. I'm interested in roles that combine AI engineering leadership, technical strategy, product architecture, technical marketing, thought leadership, analyst relations, and developer education.</p>
